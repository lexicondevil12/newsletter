Final Separation of Adient and JCI Networks

What has taken place so far and what are the next steps?

  1. On March 21st, the team successfully added security to the Extranet. All SMB, NetBios, RPC, LDAP, and DNS protocols not covered by the existing TSA between Adient and JCI were disabled. This was a pre-requisite to the final steps below and no major incidents were reported.

  2. Final separation preparation - The project team sent a list to application and server owners that appear to still have traffic traversing extranet path between Adient and JCI. We have received very little feedback (10%). The list out one final time. This is your chance to test your applications prior to separation. We encourage you to please test your applications and provide feedback.

  3. Final Testing - On April 22nd, the team will test the removal of the any to any rules. Application owners will have this LAST CHANCE to test and ensure their applications are not impacted by this change. Any issues found should follow the hyper care process below.

  4. Final Separation - If no major issues are identified on April 22nd then the final Any-to-Any rule removal will be scheduled for May 13th. Communications will be forthcoming.

Voice/Mobility Services

Accomplishments

  • The approval matrix for mobile phone ordering has been simplified. In streamlining this process we’ve vastly reduced the time it takes from ordering a mobile phone to actually receiving the mobile phone. We have also augmented this where the team can approve in case of someone being out on leave.

  • The Vodafone techfund account has been terminated in Germany. We receive ‘tech fund’ - a pool of money - against which we can order mobile phones. This is provided by Vodafone in Germany. We have a limited amount of time to use this techfund before it expires. Due to the downgraded support service from Vodafone in Germany, we have used the remainder of this ‘tech fund’ to allow the team to offer the hardware required to our users in a timely fashion. Now when a user orders a mobile phone, we can now provide one within days rather than weeks/months.

  • A reminder was added to the CONCUR and AMEX travel portals for employees traveling out of country. The reminder tells users to add an international package to their mobile phones when they travel. This should help to eliminate the high costs we see for users whilst on international travel.

Upcoming/Ongoing

  • Team members Dale Thompson, Jozef Lumtzer, and Jessica Li are comparing the Verizon Gold Car reports to the Avaya reports. Any mismatch in the data can have an impact on voice quality. Seven sites globally were identified to have this issue and we are working on correcting this to ensure voice quality remains unaffected.

  • All regions are working to add invoices for all sites globally into TEM (Telecom Expense Management) for fixed voice line reporting. This allows us to compare what we are being charged by our providers to what is in our contracts, highlight the differences, and keep track of costs on a global scale. Once fixed voice is added we will start to add network lines into the same tool.

  • Voice helpdesk tickets are in the top 10 seen by our service desk. Team member Deb Christensen is reviewing the existing helpdesk documentation. By updating these documents we will be able to reduce the amount of tickets and speed up the resolution of user issues relating to voice. 36 documents have been reviewed, updated, or deleted so far.
